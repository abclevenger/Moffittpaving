Common questions

Straight answers about paving, blacktopping, and excavation in Cashiers, Cullowhee, and the surrounding mountains.

How long before I can drive on new asphalt?

Light vehicle traffic is often OK after 24–72 hours depending on weather, mix, and thickness—we’ll give you specific guidance for your job. Stay off soft edges and avoid sharp turns while the surface is cooling.

Do you pave year-round in the mountains?

Hot-mix asphalt needs suitable temperatures and dry conditions. In western NC we schedule paving in weather windows that allow proper compaction; late fall through early spring can be limited—we’ll tell you honestly if timing should wait.

Can you overlay my existing driveway?

Sometimes yes—if the base is stable and drainage is good. Badly cracked, rutted, or soft areas often need patching or full-depth repair first. We assess the existing pavement and recommend the option that will last.

Do you offer free estimates?

Yes. Contact us with your address, approximate size, and what you need—we’ll follow up to scope the work and provide a written estimate.

What is blacktopping? Is it the same as paving?

People often say “blacktop” for the dark asphalt surface you drive on—driveways, lots, and roads. We install and repair that hot-mix asphalt surface. “Paving” is the broader term for putting down the finished layer once the base and drainage are right.
